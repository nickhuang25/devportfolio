About Me

Hi there, welcome to my website! I'm currently studying Computer Science at the University of Michigan and I enjoy building personal projects in my free time. Professionally, I've written iOS and Android applications for Ford Motor Company, developed a web-service for a F100 airline company, and worked as a Forward Deployed Software Engineer Intern at Palantir. I also conduct machine learning research to optimize terrain classification for autonomous vehicles, specializing in generative adversarial networks for image-to-image translation learning. I'm passionate about applying my software skills toward creating positive impacts on the lives of everyday people, whether that's through an app, a website, or a unique tool.

ROAHM Lab

Machine Learning Research Assistant

In my work for ROAHM Lab, I first utilized a webscraper to harvest images of different types of terrain from Google images, as well as a video scraper to capture images every 30 seconds from videos of dashboard cameras. Using these images, I trained a convolutional neural network to classify an "icy road" from other types of terrain. From there, I tuned a generative adversarial network to produce new images of icy roads, effectively testing the accuracy of the CNN. I am currently working on a novel approach to apply generic feature transfer (summer to winter) optimized for few shot learning to a small input dataset of images.

Nexecon Consulting Group

Technology Analyst

With Nexecon Consulting Group, my work as a technology analyst has allowed me to partner with several high-profile Fortune 500 clients. In one of these projects, I developed a software tool to minimize delays at an international airport by tracking previous flight data and predicting delay chance percentages for future flights, using parameters such as the gate layout. My team and I developed back-end logic as well as a front-end service in React, that is scheduled to be integrated to our client's servers in the coming months.

Ford Motor Company

Software Engineer Intern

During my time at Ford, I worked on building iOS and Android applications that could control automobile functions remotely, such as starting and stopping vehicles. The applications both utilized Oauth security measures and stored accounts and preferences locally on a device to protect privacy. They are now used internally to help developers find bugs in different Ford servers, and are used externally to showcase Ford API functionalities to potential clients.
